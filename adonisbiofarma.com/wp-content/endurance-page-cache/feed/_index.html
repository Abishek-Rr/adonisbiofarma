What exactly a cancer stage means?

Most of us would have heard the word “stage of cancer”. Have you wondered what does the word “Stage” in cancer specify? Staging is actually a way that describes the size and growth of the cancer cells in our body. By knowing what stage one is, it means to explain what size is the tumor/cell, whether it has engaged surrounding structures or any related organs too. Numerous types of examinations / screening tests may be used to find out the exact stage of cancer.  The screening might include physical examination, imaging tests, endoscopy exam, A biopsy, and laboratory testing.

Why is cancer staging important?

If there was no cancer staging system, it was going to be very confusing or problematic for doctors to decide the course of treatment as well as the screening. The staging system specifies the required details about the disease so by knowing the stage one can easily understand the severity of the disease and the prognosis. 

Types of cancer staging

The most common and widely used system of cancer staging is TNM system.  The American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC) recommends the TNM system of classification to be adopted and used by doctors.

What is TNM system of cancer staging?

TNM system a particular stage of cancer is ermined after a number or a letter is assigned to elaborate the Tumor Node and Metastasis.

T explains the tumor and its size in the body, it may be 1, 2 3, or 4 where one means denotes small and 4 means large

Image Source: Epomidicine

N describes whether the closely located lymph nodes are affected/involved. It can be anywhere between 0 and 3. Here 0 means no lymph nodes and 3 means lot of lymph nodes involved

M tells us that the cancer has spread {metastasized} to the other parts of the body. Here it is only 0 or 1, where 0 means no presence of cancer and 1 means presence of cancer.

In another clinical system of staging, there are 5 stages of cancer mentioned below:

Stage 0 : Stage 0 means no cancer or the cancer is still non invasive. Patients in stage 0 can be easily managed with the surgery alone and often show excellent prognosis. 

Stage  1  Stage one denotes the cancer or localized tumor that has not grown significantly deep into the organ or the adjacent tissues

Stage 2 and 3 Means the cancer has involved the nearby tissues and the lymph nodes. It also means that the cancer growth has started and may further spread to other organs if not treated. Based upon the number of structures involved it could be named 2 or 3.

Stage 4: This is also known as the last stage of cancer. Stage 4 means the cancer has spread to the closer as well as distant organs of the body. The prognosis of stage 4 patient is comparatively poor than of those in stage 2 and 3.

What do we mean by Restaging cancer?

A typical restaging is required during the course of treatment to re- evaluate the extent of recovery which further helps in determining the treatment process. The process of restaging and the tests which are done in restaging are quite similar to the one which were performed initially to diagnose the cancer.

The above mentioned system, of staging is actually based upon a variety of clinical examinations as wells as screening tests. So your doctor is always the best person to tell you exactly what stage is you in.

Is stage 4 cancer a death stage?

A metastatic cancer or stage 4 cancers often means that tumor has involved distant organs of body and is less likely to be cured. But there are patients who have been living with stage 4 cancer along with regular treatment. This means stage 4 patients live for many years but the recovery or prognosis is not that better as in initial stages. 

Cervical cancer staging

There are two major classification systems in cervical cancer staging.  International Federation of Gynecology and Obstetrics (FIGO) system of classification and The American Joint Committee on Cancer (AJCC) system. As per these systems cervical cancer can be staged as follows.

Tx means primary tumor can’t be assessed

T0 means there were no significant evidences of the primary tumor

Tis (TNM) means stage 0 (FIGO) and it means cancer is in pre invasive state

T1  (TNM) category means stage I (FIGO) and means that cervical cancer is limited to uterus only

T1A  (TNM) or stage IA (FIGO) means invading carcinoma has been diagnosed by microscopic or imaging screening.

T1A1 (TNM) means stage IA1 (FIGO) denotes a supporting tissue involvement not greater than 3mm in depth and 7mm in width.

T1A2 (TNM) or IA2 (FIGO) denotes supporting tissue invasion which is between 3-5mm in depth and 7 mm in width.

T1B (TNM) or IB (FIGO) denotes a clinically visible lesion involving cervix or the microscopic lesion bigger than that of T1A2/IA2

T1B1 (TNM) OR IB1 (FIGO) explains a visibly marked lesion lesser than 4cm

Immune system protect us from viruses and infection and also play important role in resistance against the development and progressive growth of tumours. An important function of immune system in the defense against cancer is inspection and recognition of foreign substances. The immune response declines with increasing age as the number and quality of immune cells decreases and its causes a higher risk of poorer outcomes.

The immune system has divided into two types:

Innate Immune System in Tumour Immunity:

Innate immune system play important role as first line of defense against pathogens and cancers. In tumors, innate immune cells are attracted into the tumor site, where they can recognize the transformed cells. Survillances of tumor cells is mediated by receptors expressed on the surface of innate immune cells, such as NK receptors, CLRs, as well as phagocytosis receptors.

White blood cells are the main type of cell responsible for protecting the body against infections. There are different types of white blood cells, and have a role in protecting the body against infections. Macrophages and mast cells recognize and destroy the target cells and initiate local tissue repair by activating vascular and fibroblast responses.

Neutrophils are playing key role to fight with infection and protecting the body from foreign antigen to cause. The other types of white blood cells (lymphocyte, monocytes) also help fight infections.

Dendritic cells: take up tumor antigens and migrate to lymphoid organs, where they processed peptides to T cells for the induction of specific antibody.

Natural killer (NK) cells have cellular cross-linking between innate and adaptive immune cells Natural killer cells have the ability to interact with dendritic cells. Natural killer cells play key role to eliminate immature dendritic cells, whereas others promote dendritic cells maturation.

Acquired immune system: The acquired immune system takes longer time to develop a plan of attack. The cells of adaptive immunity are:

B-cells: They develop and mature in the bone marrow and make proteins called antibodies that fight viruses and bacteria.

T-cells: They also form in bone marrow, but mature in the thymus. There are two main types of T-cells: helper T-cells that stimulate B-cells to make antibodies, and killer T-cells that attack cells directly.

When the immune system loses its function of recognition, tumour cells have the ability to form a tumour. Tumour cells that escape from the recognition of immune system can be explained by the following proposed mechanisms:

  • Loss or alteration of the MHC molecule

  • Tumour being immunogenic by expression of one or more antigens
  • Antigen modulation
  • Tumours do not give off inflammatory warning signals.

Can Cancer be treated with Nutrition?

Treatment of Cancer is a sequence of medications, therapy, tests, diets, proper care and timely examination. Its not singly dependent on one of the factors but is a combination of all the above listed parameters.

Out of that definitely nutrition is one of it, as its required not only during the treatment but even after patient is recovered. As Cancer may relapsed either in same organ of origin and sometimes in different organ, that’s why post cancer nutrition/diet maintenance is equally important.

Cancer leads to Anorexia and Cachexia both.

Anorexia means loss of appetite. It can happen before diagnosis as well, but its severity increases with each day/ cancer treatment. 

Cachexia means loss of weight, muscle loss and patient becomes weak. It happens due to anorexia or body not getting adequate nutrition.

Cancer medications and treatment such as Chemotherapy, Radiotherapy, Transplant, Blood transfusions, Hormone therapy, Radiation Therapy makes body weak and more prone to infections.

Nutrition is equally important as cancer treatment i.e. both should go hand in hand. Nutrition can be derived from food but for faster action many nutritionist prescribes nutrition supplements. Maintaining Haemoglobin is really necessity as to have doses of Chemotherapy, Haemoglobin should be in range, which differs from age wise and gender wise. If its not in range, doctors won’t go ahead for further dose. 

To increase Haemoglobin during Chemotherapy doctors either gives medicines or transfer of blood units as per requirement. This sometimes takes time to come to normal range and to re-start Chemotherapy. So, having proper nutrition since beginning is really helpful in maintaining it. 

For all the body requirements during and after treatment nutrient demand also changes. Before medication start, body has to fight only with cancer physically and emotionally. During treatment, fight it against dreadful disease and side effects of medicines. All individuals reacts differently with cancer medicines or we can say each one is different. Here your nutrition and healthy diet plans massive role.  

Malnutrition is another such issue which comes with Cancer which occurs due to inefficient supply and demand meets of body. As during Cancer, patient willingness to eat or appetite goes away. Once patient is malnourished, it becomes tough to fight against cancer or to have positive effect of cancer medicines. Malnutrition often leads to loss of appetite, severe weakness, paleness, fatigue, irritability, dizziness and many other weakness forms.
